Reliable. Creative. Open-minded.

I'm Janet and I'm a second-year undergrad studying mechatronics engineering at the University of Waterloo. My passion for technological design stems from using art to tell stories. I value the process of seeing an idea come to life, learning from failures, evaluating, remaining determined, collaborating on designing, developing methods of testing, succeeding, and recognizing areas for continuous improvement. As a young engineering student, I'm eager to explore my interests and gain experiences relevant to my discipline in fields of industrial automation, automotive design, and electromechanical systems. In the future, I also hope to be able to have more opportunities to work on developing electric cars.


As a high school student, I enjoyed studying math and physics. In freshman year, I joined the robotics team at my school and had the opportunity to work on an Arduino robot. Unfortunately, I only spent about three months there as my school withdrew from the competition and shut down the robotics team due to underfunding. Despite this, I became interested in programming and took a computer science course for Python the following year. Outside of school, I'm a big fan of Formula 1 racing and I like to stack cards, cook, and collect vinyl records.
